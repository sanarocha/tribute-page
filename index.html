Sylvia Plath

American poet, novelist, and short-story writer

  • Plath published her first poem at age eight. She entered and won many literary contests by a very early age
  • She entered Smith College on a scholarship in 1951 and was a cowinner of the Mademoiselle magazine fiction contest in 1952
  • At Smith College Plath achieved considerable artistic, academic, and social success, but she also suffered from severe depression, attempted suicide, and underwent a period of psychiatric hospitalization
  • Graduated from Smith with highest honours in 1955 and went on to Newnham College in Cambridge, England
  • In 1956 she married the English poet Ted Hughes; they had two children
  • Their relationship was tumultuous. Plath described, in her letters, the abuse she suffered at his hands
  • The couple separated in 1962, after Hughes’s affair with another woman
  • Plath was clinically depressed for most of her adult life, and was treated multiple times with electroconvulsive therapy (ECT)
  • Sylvia Plath’s work as a poet and expansion on the style of confessional poetry led her to become a major part of American literature
  • The ways Plath highlighted the injustices of sex-based roles and psychiatric care make her important to all of American history

For more information about Sylvia Plath, please take the time to read her Wikipedia page or one of her works.
